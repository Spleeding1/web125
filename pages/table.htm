Oil Viscosity Matters

If you’ve ever walked into an auto parts store to the engine oil isle, you’ve probably seen a vast assortment of types of engine oil. You might wonder which one is best for your car, and does it really matter.

Well, it does. There are many parts in the engine that have only a few thousandths-of-an-inch clearance between them. There are also tiny passageways that oil flows through to activate and deactivate variable-valve timing. Using an oil that is too low of a viscosity means that the thin barrier of oil may not be strong enough to keep the metal pieces from rubbing together. To high, and the oil might not be thin enough to get into the tight places it needs to. Either way, this can cause running problems and or engine failure.

Always be sure to check your owner’s manual for the correct specifications for your vehicle.
