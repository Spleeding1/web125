Oh, No!

It might not happen often, but it happens. You walk out to your car and find that you have a flat tire. Or even worse, you’re driving down the road and realize you are running on rim. What do you do?

Do you call a tow truck, or maybe phone a friend? These options can be time consuming and costly. Most manufacturers thought about this when they built your vehicle and have provided the tools you need to take care of this problem. You should always consult the owner’s manual for instructions to install the spare tire. Here is our general guidelines to get you going.

Changing a Tire

  1. Turn off vehicle and set the parking brake.
  2. Jack the vehicle.
    1. Check your owner’s manual for instructions for proper use of your jack and designated lifting points.
    2. Find the designated lifting point to place the jack.
    3. Raise the vehicle 1-2 inches. Do not lift the tire completely off the ground.
    4. Use the lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts about 1 turn.
    5. Lift the vehicle so the tire is completely off the ground.
  3. Remove the lug nuts.
  4. Remove the wheel and install the spare tire.
  5. Install the lug nuts hand-tight.
  6. Lower the vehicle until the tire is touching the ground.
  7. Using the lug wrench, tighten the lug nuts, following a cross-type pattern. If the wheel spins while tightening the lugs, lower the jack a little and try again.
  8. Completely lower the vehicle.

Safety

There are a few safety items you should be aware of while changing a tire. Please keep this in mind if you have to change one.

Location

When changing a tire, you want to park your vehicle on level ground. If you are parked on an incline, you increase the risk of having the vehicle fall off the jack. Depending on where you are if it falls, you could be injured in the process, not to mention potentional damage to your vehicle.

Lug Nuts

Be sure that the tire is firmly contacting the ground when the initial loosening and final tightening occurs. If the tire is completely off the ground, the tire might just spin and the lug nut might never come loose, or might not be tight enough and the wheel could fall off later. If it doesn’t spin, the force of breaking it loose could knock the vehicle off the jack.
